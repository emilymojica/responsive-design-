Taking care of your mind is important to functioning in everyday life.


It’s caring for your mind, making sure that your stress levels are low, that you are regulating your emotions, and that you know how to cope when something goes wrong.

It is also making sure that you are spiritually healthy is important. Doing things you love and feeling as if you have a purpose in life are examples of good spiritual health.


When we hear psychological self care, we often think of staying home and binging on Netflix or going on walks instead of doing responsibilities.

Always remember that self care is doing things that both FEEL good and are ACTUALLY good for you. While watching all of your favorite shows in one sitting may feel good in the moment, you might feel lethargic and awful later, which can border on self harm instead of self care.

Caring for yourself psychologically means that you are making sure that you are balancing negative feelings and positive feelings, not trying to bury negative feelings or doing things that make you feel good but make you feel bad after.

Improving your mental health is not just about being happy all the time, it means taking care of your mind all the time, whether you feel good or bad.

Your mind is your most powerful yet your most fragile organ. For this reason, it is the organ that requires the most care. Your mental health can be affected by nearly everything: your own physical health, your environment, your friends and family, even your job.

Sometimes our minds aren’t as flawless as we like them to be. We get stressed out, we have emotions that are either too strong or too weak, and we often suffer from symptoms of anxiety and depression, which can continue to get worse.

Some symptoms of anxiety and depression are:

    - restlessness

    - fatigue

    - lack of focus

    - irritability

    - mood swings

These symptoms often are a sign of poor emotional wellness.

So how can you improve your emotional wellness? First off, you can start a journal to start documenting the way you feel. Or, whenever you can, just create something that expresses the way you feel, like poem or a drawing. Talking to someone, like a family member, a friend, a therapist, or a doctor can also help you find ways to start improving your emotional health.

Another way you can start emotional self care is by creating a mood tracker. A mood tracker is a form in which you can document how you felt during your day, just by filling in a box. With this mood tracker template, all you have to do is fill in the box that matches the date with the color that represents the way you feel.

While these methods may help with smaller scale emotional issues, there is a possibility that, if you have consistently poor emotional wellness, you may have clinical depression, which is characterized by constant symptoms of depression and anxiety, along with suicidal thoughts, which can be life threatening.

If you think you are a loved one is suffering from clinical depression, please visit Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration
