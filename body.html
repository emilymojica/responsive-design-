Physical self care is the most basic form of self care.


It’s caring for your body, making sure you are getting enough nutrition, sleep, and exercise.

Physical self care does not just mean things you need on a physical level, just things that make you feel good, like going to the spa or using beauty products.


When you hear physical self care, you might think about extreme diets and exercise plans, and other similar things.

Remember that these things are NOT self care. If anything, participating in such drastic activities may not only be detrimental to your physical health, but your psychological and practical health as well.

Caring for yourself means improving yourself while also making sure that you are comfortable and healthy. Exercise is always good, but make sure to not hurt yourself or overwork yourself. Try not to binge on food, but don’t negate your need for food either.

Physical health is always about balance, and making sure that you are your best self.

Physical self care is not limited to your basic needs. Doing things that make you feel good can also be considered self care. Using beauty and hygiene products or being in a clean environment. in other ways is an important part of your wellness.

A beauty routine is a good way to practice the beauty therapy method of self care. Feeling clean and hygienic can help increase your wellness. Hygiene is vital to your health, and it can go hand in hand with beauty to make you feel like your best self.

Some things you can add to a hygiene self care routine are:

    - bubbles baths and detox baths

    - face masks and scrubs

    - skin care or hair care routines

    - makeup

    - use of essential oils and soaps

You can use these suggestions, but do whatever makes you feel good!

The benefits of beauty therapy are numerous. Beauty therapy can lead to stress reduction, less negative thought patterns, building of good habits, and it can increase self esteem, self love, self reflection and self expression. The most important part of participating in beauty self care is to feel clean and healthy. Remember that it is not frivolous or selfish to do participate in beauty therapy, it is caring for yourself and your needs.

It may be difficult to figure out what you would like to add to your hygiene therapy routine. Maybe the suggestions on this page don’t suit you, or you can’t seem to find something that works. If so, use this graphic organizer to see what is best for you. Write about your daily routine in the first column, and write how it makes you feel in the second. This will help you put into perspective to see what helps you and what does not.

While beauty therapy is important to self esteem and self care, always remember to never go too far. Focusing too much on beauty and appearance can lead you down the slippery slope of eating disorders, conditions that are caused by extremely negative body image and doing things like fasting or purging to control weight.

If you think you or a loved one may have an eating disorder, please visit The National Eating Disorder Helpline
