Self care is not just about how caring for your body and your mind, but also caring for the way that you interact with the world.


Practical self care is caring for yourself throughout your daily life, for example, doing things that make you happy and making sure you have boundaries for yourself so you donâ€™t get overworked.

Staying organized and being proud of your work are vital parts of practical self care, but there is a difference between being practical and being a perfectionist.

If practical self care is bringing you more stress than it relieves, always remember that self care is not about striving for perfection; it is about becoming the best version of you.

Practical self care is important to functioning in daily life, and it can also be a stress reliever. Seeing a workspace organized or successfully managing your budget can make you feel good about yourself, and it can reduce any anxiety about things you have to get done.

Caring for the way that you live your life every day is an important part of caring for yourself.

Interacting with others is a normal part of daily life. Whether it be work, school, restaurants, stores, and other places, you can expect to see other people. Whether you are socially awkward or socially confident, managing your social life is important to your self care.

Practicing social self care does not mean that you have to be a social butterfly. Rather, social self care means having good communication skills. Not only that, social self care is also recognizing toxic relationships, and leaving relationships that are harming you.

Some examples of good communication skills are:

    - actively listening

    - asking questions

    - speaking clearly

    - being compassionate

    - providing feedback

    - being trusting

Good communication skills can help you during your daily life.

How can you improve your communication skills? The primary thing you can do is practice. Talking with other people and finding what you need to practice in is a great way to hone your skills. Identifying toxic relationships and friendships in your life is important. Some hallmarks of a toxic relationship are a lack of trust, a hostile atmosphere, and judgement, along other things.

A way you can visualize your social self care is with this graphic organizer. For each social skill listed on the worksheet, rate yourself on how confident you are in that skill. Optionally, you can give this worksheet to friends and family members so see what they would do. This can help you put your communication skills into perspective.

A toxic relationship is harmful to your health, and it is best to get out of it as soon as possible. However, a relationship can morph from toxic to violent and abusive. Not only that, they may be hard to escape without a fear of violence. An abusive relationship is not only a threat to your mental health, but your safety as well.
